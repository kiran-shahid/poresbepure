Skin Type Guide

Oily Skin

Oily skin is characterized by an overproduction of sebum, resulting in a shiny complexion and enlarged pores. People with oily skin may be more prone to acne breakouts.

Skincare Tips for Oily Skin:

  • Use a gentle cleanser to remove excess oil.
  • Avoid heavy and greasy moisturizers.
  • Use oil-free or non-comedogenic products.
  • Consider using a mattifying primer or powder to control shine.
  • Keep your skin hydrated with a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer.

Skincare Tips for Different Weather Conditions:

  • In hot and humid weather, use oil-free and lightweight products.
  • In cold and dry weather, use a moisturizer with added hydration.

Additional Tips:

  • Avoid touching your face frequently to prevent the transfer of excess oil.
  • Practice good hygiene by washing your face twice a day.
  • Incorporate fruits and vegetables rich in antioxidants into your diet.

Dry Skin

Dry skin is characterized by a lack of moisture, resulting in a tight and sometimes flaky complexion. People with dry skin may experience itching and discomfort.

Skincare Tips for Dry Skin:

  • Use a gentle cleanser without harsh ingredients.
  • Apply a rich and hydrating moisturizer regularly.
  • Avoid hot showers or baths as they can strip the skin of natural oils.
  • Consider using a humidifier to add moisture to the air.
  • Protect your skin from harsh weather conditions with a nourishing facial oil or balm.

Skincare Tips for Different Weather Conditions:

  • In hot and dry weather, use a lightweight moisturizer with added hydration.
  • In cold and windy weather, use a thicker moisturizer or facial oil to protect the skin.

Additional Tips:

  • Avoid over-exfoliating as it can further dry out the skin.

Combination Skin

Combination skin is characterized by having both oily and dry areas on the face. The T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) tends to be oily, while the cheeks and other areas may be normal to dry.
